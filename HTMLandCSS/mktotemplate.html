Marketo AE's- What you need to know

For any type of automated campaign you wish to create with Marketo, various types of content are needed to power those efforts. To that end, our platform gives users the ability to create (1) Emails, (2) Landing Pages, (3) Forms, and (4) Push Notifications. By the end of reading this page, you will be able to understand and articulate to prosects:

The marketing and business value of Marketo assets

How Marketo helps non-technical marketers personalize these assets to maximize engagement

Provide answers to the most commonly asked questions about what Marketo assets can/cannot do

Asset: Email

Email is the primary asset most Marketo customers leverage to power their campaigns. In today's digital age, its the primary way business' communicate with their customers. Marketo's What You See Is What You Get (WYSIWYG for short) editor makes it easy for non-technical marketers to (1) create and (2) customize their emails. In terms of creation, our template library has over 40 different email designs to choose from which can be imported directly into their arrount. They are all responsive, so you can easily see how they would look on a variety of devices. In terms of customization, leveraging (1) tokens and (2) dynamic content are the way to maximize engagement with recipients. Think of a token as a placeholder for a field within the database, such as "lead.firstName". Tokens help us personalize how we communicate with our audience AT SCALE. Similarly, Dynamic content takes personaliztion to the next level by rendering different versions of an individual email based upon a characteristic of the recipient. Asset: Landing Page

A landing page is a web page that allows you to capture a visitor's information through a lead-capture form (AKA a conversion form). A good landing page will target a particular audience, such as traffic from an email campaign promoting a particular ebook, or visitors who click on a pay-per-click ad promoting your webinar. Creating landing pages allows you to target your audience, offer them something of value, and convert a higher percentage of your visitors into leads, while also capturing information about who they are and what they've converted on. Marketo's landing pages are built using a pure drag-and-drop interface, while also leveraging the tokens and dynamic content features for personalization.

Asset: Form

Forms are the way you gather information from your leads. They are a critical component of any landing page, but what you ask on these forms are perhaps more critical. You don't want to scare away potential leads by asking irrelevant questions, or questions that they aren't yet prepared to answer. Building trust between your brand and your visitors is key to gathering the information you need. Start building that trust through your Top of Funnel offerings; your forms should ask enough information to find out some key pieces of who they are, what they do, and what they are looking for, so you can better segment your leads into personas.

Marketo AE's, FAQ on DIY Design

Q. How do you make emails dynamic?
  • A. Apply a segment to the body of text or image you’d like to apply dynamic content to (this is the same for landing pages).
Q. How do you create a segment for dynamic content?
  • A. Dynamic Content segments are created in the lead database. There is a limit of 20 segmentations and each segmentation has a limit of 100 variations (i.e. a Product Interest segment can contain 100 different products).
Q. What fields are available as tokens?
  • A. Any field in Marketo - so if you synced your entire SF database in Marketo, all those fields can be used as tokens.
Q. What’s the difference between a landing page vs. microsite?
  • A. A landing page only has one clear call to action which is a form fill - the ultimate goal of a landing page is a form conversion. A microsite has other navigation links on them, like a mini website.
Q. Why is progressive profiling important?
  • A. Progressive profiling allows you to capture additional data without having the lead fill out the same fields on a form they’ve already filled out (form pre-fill). Ultimately, you want to make the form fill as easy and quick as possible so that someone doesn’t abandon the form.
Q. Can I take Marketo Forms and place it on non-Marketo landing pages / my own website?
  • A. Yes. Marketo’s forms come with embed codes you can place anywhere on your own landing pages or your website. HOWEVER, you will not be able to get progressive profiling or form pre-fill capabilities using a marketo form on a non-marketo landing page (you CAN through iframes).

Marketo SC's, FAQ on DIY Design

Q. How do I upload my own html template into Marketo?
  • A. In the Design Studio, in the “new” drop down menu, select “ new email template” (NOT “new email”), and copy and paste your html (this is the same for landing pages).
Q. What is a transactional / operational email?
  • A. Transactional emails are sent as a reaction to an action taken by someone such as: order confirmation, password reset, signup welcome email..etc. These will ignore unsubscribes and marketing suspended list.
Q. How does Marketo ensure email deliverability?
  • A. We have a dedicated team actively monitoring email deliverability. Check out trust.marketo.com and see our Sender Score (which is an email reputation metric).
Q. How do you make only certain fields of the form show up depending on a response to a previous field?
  • A. Visibility rules - you can set up logic within each field on the form to either have the field show up or hide dependent on a previous field’s data.
Q. Why are hidden fields important on forms?
  • A. Hidden fields do not show up on forms (the enduser cannot see them): this allows you to capture additional data about that person or where they came from (i.e. PPC campaigns - you are able to capture UTM parameters).
